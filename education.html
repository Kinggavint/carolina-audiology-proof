Five signs it is time for a hearing evaluation

Many adults wait years before getting their hearing tested. Often it is a spouse, a friend, or a colleague who notices first. Here are the signs we hear most often from patients on their first visit.

You turn up the TV. Family members ask you to lower the volume. You raise it again when they leave the room.

Conversations in noisy places feel exhausting. Restaurants, family dinners, church gatherings, and group meetings start to feel like work.

You ask people to repeat themselves often. Especially women's and children's voices.

You miss parts of phone calls. You catch some words but lose the thread.

You feel more tired after social events. Hearing harder is real work, and it adds up.

If any of these sound familiar, a diagnostic hearing evaluation will give you a clear picture and a path forward.

What to expect at your first appointment

Your first appointment is about getting clear answers in a relaxed setting. Here is what a typical visit looks like.

Conversation. We ask about your hearing, your medical history, and your lifestyle. You bring a family member if you want a second set of ears.

Examination. We look in your ears to check for wax, infection, or anything else that needs attention.

Testing. Comprehensive diagnostic testing measures how well you hear pure tones, how well you understand speech, and how your middle ear is functioning.

Results. We walk you through your audiogram in plain language, with your family in the room if you brought them.

Plan. If you have hearing loss, we talk through your options. If you do not, we say so. Either way, you leave with a clear next step.

Hearing technology has come a long way

The hearing aids your parents wore are not the hearing technology we fit today. Modern devices are smaller, smarter, and far better at telling speech apart from background noise.

Speech clarity. Today's processors separate voices from background sound in ways that were impossible even five years ago.

Connectivity. Stream phone calls, video calls, music, and TV audio directly to your hearing technology.

Discretion. Many of the devices we fit from Oticon and ReSound are barely visible.

Rechargeable. Most patients are choosing rechargeable styles, which means no more tiny batteries.

If you tried hearing aids years ago and felt let down, the technology you would try today is a very different experience.
